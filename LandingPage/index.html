The Problem

THE KODAIKANAL
PARADIGM

It's monsoon season in Kodaikanal. Dense cloud canopy, steep ghats, and a tourist influx that overwhelms every cell tower within 40 kilometers. The UPI system — India's digital payment miracle — goes silent.

A chai vendor stares at his QR code. A tourist stares at a payment timeout. Both have everything they need to complete the transaction — loaded accounts, willing parties — but no signal. The digital payment collapses.

"The merchant has a UPI QR. The tourist has a bank account.
But between them: 0 bars of signal, and ₹35 that can't move."

This is India's digital payment paradox. UPI reaches 450 million users but fails exactly where the physical world demands it most — high altitude tourist zones, dense forest markets, monsoon-flooded rural corridors.

NPCI's UPI Lite attempted this with NFC-based offline micro-transactions. But it requires NFC hardware, bank backend settlement, and a central server that itself needs periodic connectivity. Change the mountain. Don't change the server.

AirWeave solves this at the cryptographic layer. No bank server. No NFC chip requirement. No central authority. Just mathematics, running locally on the device in your pocket.

The Solution

HOW IT WORKS

OFFLINE
STEP 01
VOICE CAPTURE
User speaks in Hindi, Tamil, or English. "bhaiya ko do 35 rupaye." Moonshine ONNX model runs entirely on-device via WebGPU — no cloud, no API call, no signal required.
→
OFFLINE
STEP 02
INTENT PARSE
Sarvam 105B processes the transcription and extracts structured payment intent: { amount: 35, currency: "INR" }. Falls back to regex parser for pure offline mode. No hallucination risk on numerics.
OFFLINE
STEP 03
BIOMETRIC AUTH
WebAuthn passkey triggers device biometric — Face ID, fingerprint, or Windows Hello. Private key never leaves the device's secure enclave. Same security model as Apple Pay. Fully offline.
→
OFFLINE
STEP 04
EIP-712 SIGN
Device signs a typed cryptographic voucher: { from, to, amountINR: 3500, nonce: 7, expiry }. Pure secp256k1 elliptic curve math. The signature is a mathematical IOU — unforgeable without the private key.
LOCAL P2P
STEP 05
HOTSPOT RELAY
Signed voucher POSTs to vendor's local server at 192.168.x.x:3000 over WiFi hotspot. Sub-200ms transfer. Zero internet. The vendor's device runs ecrecover locally to verify the signature is mathematically valid.
→
LOCAL
STEP 06
SQLITE ESCROW
Verified voucher is committed to vendor's append-only SQLite ledger. Vendor screen shows "₹35.00 PENDING." The signed voucher is the cryptographic proof — valid forever, redeemable on any connection.
ON CONNECTIVITY
STEP 07 — FINAL SETTLEMENT
MONAD PARALLEL BATCH SETTLEMENT
When the vendor's device detects signal, MonadAirVault contract receives the pending voucher batch. Queries live USD/INR price oracle. Calculates exact USDX required to honor the INR promise. Monad's parallel EVM processes all independent vouchers simultaneously — no state contention, no queue. Entire regional backlog settles inside a single 0.4-second block. Vendor sees: "₹35.00 SETTLED ✓"

Trust Model

FRAUD-PROOF

01
SIGNATURE FORGERY IMPOSSIBLE
A signed EIP-712 voucher can only be created by the holder of the customer's private key, stored exclusively in the device secure enclave. Intercepting the WiFi payload gives you an unmodifiable blob.
ecrecover(Hash_EIP712, v, r, s) → address
if recovered ≠ voucher.from → REJECTED
02
NONCE PREVENTS REPLAY
Every voucher carries a strictly incrementing nonce. The smart contract tracks every nonce it has processed per address. Submit the same signed voucher twice and the second attempt reverts: "nonce already used."
require(usedNonces[from][nonce] == false)
usedNonces[from][nonce] = true
03
OVER-COLLATERALIZED VAULT
Before going offline, users pre-deposit USDX into MonadAirVault. The app caps offline spending at 60% of vault balance — permanent buffer against rate volatility between signing and settlement.
max_offline_spend = vault_balance × 0.60
voucher.amountINR → converted at settlement rate
04
DYNAMIC INR SETTLEMENT
The vendor's INR promise is locked at signing time. USDX calculation happens on-chain at settlement using a live price oracle — meaning the vendor always receives exactly the promised rupee value, regardless of market movement.
usdx = (amountINR × 1e18) / currentUsdInrRate
vendor guaranteed: ₹ amount = constant
